MNHEME - a personal diary to understand how you think

Last week Mirko Perrone released an interesting project: 𝗠𝗡𝗛𝗘𝗠𝗘, a database inspired by how human memory works.

Details in his post: https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7440670399576760320/

I liked the idea so much that I translated the logic from Python (built for backend) to JavaScript and added a few tweaks to turn it into a PWA: a personal diary for your thoughts.

Key sections:

- Perceive: turns raw thoughts into structured memories
- Memories: browse and search through all your saved memories
- Brain: MNHEME's cognitive engine, query, reflect, dream, analyze
- Stats: database statistics, emotional timeline and connection graph
- Setting: customize your diary and configure one or more LLMs to power it

What about security?

Top notch, everything is saved locally 😂
(but you can import/export data across devices)

Try it here: https://mnheme-musa.web.app/

If you give it a try, let me know what you think, any feedback is valuable for both me and Mirko Perrone to improve the project.
